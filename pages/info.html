Quartz

A quartz crystal is a crystalline form of silicon dioxide (SiO2), a common mineral found in the Earth's crust. It's known for its unique piezoelectric properties, meaning it can generate an electrical charge when subjected to mechanical stress, and vice versa. This property makes it vital in electronics like watches and radios.

Grape Agate

Grape agate is a type of botryoidal purple chalcedony, not technically an agate, that forms in clusters resembling grapes. It's known for its unique appearance and is found primarily in Indonesia. While often called "grape agate," the mineralogical name is actually botryoidal purple chalcedony.

Jade

Jade is a term for two different silicate minerals, jadeite and nephrite, known for their durability and ornamental value. While often associated with green, jade can also be found in white, yellow, black, and even lavender colors. Both jadeite and nephrite are mineral aggregates, meaning they are composed of interlocking crystals, rather than single mineral species.

Apatite

Apatite is a group of phosphate minerals, and apatite crystals are formed when these minerals crystallize. They are known for their diverse range of colors, including blue, green, yellow, and purple, and are often found in various rock types. Apatite crystals are relatively common but can be challenging to identify due to their resemblance to other minerals.
